1. PRODUCT OVERVIEW
=============================================================

The Horizon Conservative Income Fund (HCIF) is designed for capital
preservation with modest income generation. The fund allocates
75-90% of assets to high-grade government and corporate debt
instruments, with the remaining 10-25% in large-cap blue-chip
equities to provide a measured inflation hedge. The fund was built
specifically for RMs serving clients whose primary objective is
protecting accumulated wealth while still participating modestly
in long-term capital markets growth, rather than maximizing return.

Inception Date: 2014-03-01
Fund Size (AUM): USD 1.42 billion (as of last quarter)
Benchmark: Composite Conservative Index (70% Bloomberg Aggregate
Bond Index / 30% S&P 500 Low Volatility Index)
Base Currency: USD
Minimum Initial Investment: USD 5,000
Minimum Additional Investment: USD 500
Fund Domicile: Luxembourg (UCITS-compliant structure)
Fund Administrator: Horizon Fund Services Ltd.
Custodian: Northbridge Trust & Custody Bank
Independent Auditor: Halliday & Marsh LLP

The fund has maintained a consistent mandate since inception, with
only minor adjustments to the equity sleeve's volatility-screening
methodology in 2019 and 2023. No change to the fund's core
objective or risk classification has occurred since launch.

2. INVESTMENT OBJECTIVE AND PHILOSOPHY
=============================================================

To generate stable income with low volatility while preserving
capital over a rolling 3-year horizon. The fund is not designed
for aggressive capital appreciation.

The portfolio management team applies a "capital-first" philosophy:
every security considered for inclusion is first screened for
downside protection characteristics before being evaluated for
yield or income contribution. This is a deliberate inversion of
the more common "yield-first" approach used by many income funds,
and is the primary reason HCIF has historically exhibited a lower
maximum drawdown than comparable category peers during periods of
market stress.

The philosophy rests on three pillars:

Pillar One — Quality Over Yield: The team will forgo incremental
yield if it requires meaningfully reducing average credit quality.
The fund's investment committee has a standing policy that no more
than 8% of the bond sleeve may carry a rating below BBB-.

Pillar Two — Duration Discipline: Effective duration is actively
managed within a 3.5 to 5.5 year band, intentionally avoiding the
temptation to extend duration purely to capture a steeper yield
curve, since duration extension increases interest rate sensitivity
beyond what the fund's conservative mandate permits.

Pillar Three — Equity as Ballast, Not Growth Engine: The equity
sleeve is constructed using a low-volatility factor screen, favoring
companies with stable earnings, lower beta, and consistent dividend
histories over high-growth names. This sleeve exists to provide a
long-term inflation hedge and modest total return enhancement, not
to drive the fund's overall return profile.

3. ASSET ALLOCATION RANGES AND SUB-STRATEGY BREAKDOWN
=============================================================

Strategic (long-term target) allocation:
- Government & Agency Bonds: 35% - 50% (target midpoint: 42%)
- Investment-Grade Corporate Bonds (BBB- and above): 25% - 40%
  (target midpoint: 32%)
- Large-Cap Equities (low-volatility factor): 10% - 25%
  (target midpoint: 18%)
- Cash & Cash Equivalents: 0% - 10% (target midpoint: 4%)
- Other (limited allocation to inflation-linked securities): up to 4%

Sub-strategy breakdown within the government & agency sleeve:
- Sovereign bonds (developed markets): approximately 65% of sleeve
- Supranational and agency issuance: approximately 25% of sleeve
- Inflation-linked sovereign bonds: approximately 10% of sleeve

Sub-strategy breakdown within the corporate bond sleeve:
- Financials (banks, insurers): approximately 30% of sleeve
- Industrials: approximately 25% of sleeve
- Utilities and infrastructure-adjacent issuers: approximately 20%
  of sleeve
- Consumer staples and healthcare: approximately 25% of sleeve

Sub-strategy breakdown within the equity sleeve:
- Consumer staples: approximately 22% of sleeve
- Healthcare: approximately 20% of sleeve
- Utilities: approximately 18% of sleeve
- Financials (large, diversified): approximately 15% of sleeve
- Industrials (defensive subsegment): approximately 13% of sleeve
- Other defensive sectors: approximately 12% of sleeve

Rebalancing occurs on a quarterly cycle, with tactical adjustments
permitted within stated bands at the portfolio manager's discretion
in response to material shifts in the rate environment or credit
cycle. Any rebalancing outside the stated strategic bands requires
investment committee approval and is disclosed in the fund's
quarterly factsheet.

4. PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT TEAM AND PROCESS
=============================================================

The fund is managed by the Horizon Fixed Income & Conservative
Strategies desk, a team that also oversees the firm's institutional
conservative mandates. The desk follows a four-stage process:

Stage 1 — Macro Framework: The team establishes a house view on
rates, credit spreads, and inflation expectations, refreshed
monthly and reviewed weekly for material developments.

Stage 2 — Security Selection: Bottom-up credit analysis is applied
to all corporate bond candidates, with a dedicated credit research
function independent of the portfolio management team to avoid
conflicts of interest in security selection.

Stage 3 — Portfolio Construction: Securities are blended according
to the strategic allocation targets, with explicit attention to
correlation between the bond and equity sleeves during periods of
market stress, since the fund's defensive characteristics depend
on the two sleeves not moving in lockstep during a drawdown.

Stage 4 — Risk Oversight: An independent risk team, separate from
portfolio management, monitors duration, credit quality, sector
concentration, and equity beta against policy limits daily, with
formal escalation procedures if any metric breaches a soft limit
for more than five consecutive business days.

5. RISK CHARACTERISTICS — DETAILED
=============================================================

Risk Rating: LOW TO MODERATE (Internal Scale 2 of 5)
Standard Deviation (3-yr trailing): 4.8%
Standard Deviation (5-yr trailing): 5.3%
Maximum Drawdown (5-yr lookback): -7.3% (March 2026 rate shock)
Maximum Drawdown (10-yr lookback): -9.1% (2020 market dislocation)
Duration (effective): 4.2 years
Convexity: Positive, moderate
Credit Quality: 92% of bond holdings rated A- or higher; 8% rated
BBB+ to BBB-
Equity Beta (sleeve only, vs. S&P 500): 0.71
Sharpe Ratio (5-yr trailing, illustrative): 0.84
Sortino Ratio (5-yr trailing, illustrative): 1.12

Risk factors in detail:

Interest Rate Risk: As a fund with material exposure to fixed-rate
debt instruments, HCIF's bond sleeve will lose value when interest
rates rise and gain value when rates fall, all else equal. The
fund's effective duration of 4.2 years implies that a 100 basis
point parallel rise in rates would be expected to reduce the bond
sleeve's value by approximately 4.2%, before accounting for any
offsetting carry or convexity effects.

Credit Risk: While the fund maintains a high average credit
quality, individual issuer downgrades or defaults remain possible.
The fund's credit research process is designed to identify
deteriorating credit profiles before they result in a rating
downgrade, but this process cannot eliminate credit risk entirely.

Equity Market Risk: The equity sleeve, while constructed using a
low-volatility screen, remains subject to broad equity market
declines. During periods of severe, broad-based market stress,
defensive equity sectors have historically still declined, albeit
typically less than the broader market.

Liquidity Risk: Under normal market conditions, the fund's
underlying holdings are highly liquid. During periods of severe
market dislocation, bid-ask spreads on corporate bonds in
particular can widen materially, which may affect the fund's
ability to transact at expected prices, though this risk is
mitigated by the fund's cash buffer and high-quality government
bond holdings.

Currency Risk: The fund is predominantly USD-denominated, with
limited (under 5%) non-USD exposure, which is generally hedged
back to USD. Residual currency risk is considered immaterial to
the fund's overall risk profile.

This fund carries interest rate risk, credit risk, and limited
equity market risk. It is NOT principal-protected and the unit
value can fall, although historically with lower magnitude than
balanced or growth-oriented funds.

=============================================================

The following commentary describes illustrative historical
performance periods for RM context. All figures are historical
and are not indicative of future results. Past performance is
not forward-looking guidance.

to broad market bond aggregates helped limit downside in the bond

was not immune to losses.

Period C — Recovery and Stabilization (illustrative): Following
periods of market stress, the fund has historically participated
in a portion of subsequent market recoveries, reflecting its
partial equity allocation, while lagging more aggressive funds
during sharp upward market moves, consistent with its conservative
mandate.

RMs should never present these illustrative period descriptions as
specific forward guidance or as a guarantee of how the fund would
behave in a future similar scenario. Markets do not repeat
identically, and the fund's holdings and risk parameters may have
changed since any historical period referenced.

7. SUITABILITY GUIDANCE — DETAILED SCENARIOS
=============================================================

Recommended Investor Risk Profile: Conservative or Balanced
Recommended Holding Period: Minimum 3 years

NOT suitable for:
  - Investors requiring guaranteed return of principal
  - Investors with an Aggressive or Growth risk profile seeking
    higher capital appreciation (allocate to HBGF or HAEF instead)
  - Investors needing same-day liquidity (settlement is T+3)
  - Investors whose sole objective is maximizing tax-deferred
    growth over a multi-decade horizon (a Growth-oriented vehicle
    is typically more appropriate)

This fund IS generally suitable for:
  - Retirees or near-retirees seeking income with limited drawdown
  - Conservative clients building an emergency-adjacent reserve
    beyond standard cash holdings
  - Balanced clients seeking a defensive sleeve within a broader
    multi-asset portfolio
  - Clients transitioning out of a Growth or Aggressive profile
    following a documented life-stage change (e.g., approaching
    retirement) who need a lower-volatility holding period bridge

Illustrative scenario A: A Conservative-profile client aged 68,
recently retired, seeking monthly income to supplement a pension,
with a stated need to preserve principal. HCIF's monthly
distribution mechanism and low historical drawdown make it a
reasonable core holding for discussion, subject to the standard
suitability check against the client's full RPQ.

Illustrative scenario B: A Balanced-profile client aged 45 with a
10-year horizon for a portion of their portfolio, seeking to
reduce overall portfolio volatility after a market stress event
prompted them to reassess their risk tolerance. HCIF may be
appropriate as a partial reallocation target, subject to the
client's updated RPQ reflecting any change in stated risk
tolerance.

Illustrative scenario C: An Aggressive-profile client in their 30s
asking about HCIF purely because "it sounds safer." This is a
scenario where the RM should probe further — if the client's
underlying objective is long-term growth and their risk profile is
accurately assessed as Aggressive, HCIF is unlikely to be
appropriate as anything beyond a small tactical cash-equivalent
allocation, and the RM should not steer the client away from their
documented risk profile without a formal re-assessment.

9. DISTRIBUTION POLICY AND INCOME MECHANICS
=============================================================

Income distributions are declared monthly, subject to
distributable surplus. Distribution Reinvestment Plan (DRIP)
available at no additional charge.

Trailing 12-month distribution yield: 3.9% (not guaranteed;
historical figures are not indicative of future distributions)

Distribution mechanics: The fund's distributable income is
calculated based on net investment income (interest and dividend
income received, less fund expenses) for the relevant period.
Distributions are not guaranteed and may be reduced, suspended, or
in rare circumstances funded partially from capital if net
investment income is insufficient to support the historical
distribution rate; any such instance would be disclosed in the
fund's monthly factsheet.

Distribution payment timeline: Declared distributions are typically
paid within 5 business days of the month-end record date, either
as cash to the client's nominated account or reinvested as
additional units under the DRIP election.

10. TAX CONSIDERATIONS (GENERAL, NOT ADVICE)
=============================================================

Distributions may be taxed as income in the investor's
jurisdiction of residence. Capital gains on units held longer
than 12 months may qualify for long-term treatment in applicable
jurisdictions. RMs must direct clients to a qualified tax advisor
for jurisdiction-specific guidance; RMs are not authorized to
provide tax advice.

General considerations RMs may reference at a high level (subject
to the licensing gate in the Suitability & Compliance Policy):

- The fund's Luxembourg UCITS domicile may have different
  withholding tax implications than a domestically-domiciled fund,
  depending on the client's tax residency and any applicable tax
  treaty.
- Reinvested distributions under the DRIP election are still
  generally treated as taxable income in most jurisdictions in the
  year they are declared, notwithstanding that no cash was
  received by the client; clients should be made aware of this
  general principle and referred to their tax advisor for
  confirmation in their specific jurisdiction.
- Exit load charged on early redemption is generally not tax
  deductible as an investment expense in most jurisdictions, though
  this varies and must be confirmed by a qualified tax professional.

11. LIQUIDITY & REDEMPTION MECHANICS
=============================================================

Redemption Frequency: Daily (on business days)
Settlement: Trade Date + 3 Business Days (T+3)
Cut-off Time for Same-Day Pricing: 3:00 PM fund domicile time
Redemption Gate: The fund reserves the right to limit redemptions
to 10% of NAV per dealing day during periods of severe market
stress, per the Fund's offering memorandum section 8.4.

Partial redemptions are permitted, subject to a minimum residual
holding of USD 1,000 to keep the account open; redemptions that
would bring the account below this threshold are treated as a
full redemption and the account is closed.

In-kind redemptions are not available to retail-channel clients
and require Investment Committee approval if requested by an
institutional client.

13. COMPARISON TO RELATED HORIZON PRODUCTS
=============================================================

HCIF vs. Horizon Balanced Growth Fund (HBGF): HCIF carries a
materially lower equity allocation (10-25% vs. 50-70%), lower
historical volatility, and a lower historical maximum drawdown,
but correspondingly lower long-term expected return. HBGF is
appropriate for Balanced or Growth profile clients with a longer
horizon; HCIF is appropriate for Conservative or Balanced profile
clients prioritizing capital preservation.

HCIF vs. Fixed Deposits / Bond Ladder (PG-004 products): Fixed
Deposits offer principal protection (subject to issuer risk) but
no daily liquidity and typically lower yield than HCIF's blended
return profile over a full market cycle. The Bond Ladder offers
more granular control over maturity and duration but requires
more active client engagement than a single diversified fund.
HCIF may be appropriate for clients seeking a single, professionally
managed conservative holding rather than self-directed duration
management.

HCIF vs. Money Market Notes (PG-004): Money market notes offer
greater liquidity and lower volatility than HCIF but a materially
lower expected long-term return, and offer essentially no
inflation-hedging equity component. Money market notes are more
appropriate for near-term liquidity needs (under 12 months); HCIF
is more appropriate for a 3+ year conservative allocation.

14. FREQUENTLY ASKED CLIENT QUESTIONS (RM REFERENCE)
=============================================================

Q: "Is my money guaranteed in this fund?"
A: No. RMs should clearly state that HCIF is not principal-
protected and that, while historically lower volatility than
balanced or growth funds, the unit value can decline.

Q: "Why does this fund hold any equities at all if it's
conservative?"
A: The equity sleeve exists to provide a measured inflation hedge
and modest total return enhancement; it is constructed using a
low-volatility screen specifically to limit its contribution to
overall fund volatility.

Q: "Can I get my money out quickly if I need it?"
A: Redemptions settle T+3 under normal conditions. RMs should
disclose the redemption gate provision for periods of severe
market stress per Section 11.

Q: "What happens to my distributions if I don't need the income?"
A: Clients can elect the Distribution Reinvestment Plan (DRIP) at
no additional cost, which automatically reinvests distributions as
additional fund units.

Q: "How does this compare to just holding a fixed deposit?"
A: Refer to Section 13's comparison; RMs should present both
options factually and let the client's documented risk profile and
liquidity needs guide the discussion, rather than advocating for
one product over the other absent a completed suitability
assessment.

16. OPERATIONAL DUE DILIGENCE SUMMARY
=============================================================

The following operational due diligence points are maintained for
RM reference and may be shared with institutional or sophisticated
clients upon request, subject to Compliance pre-clearance for any
non-standard disclosure.

Valuation Policy: The fund is priced daily using independent
third-party pricing sources for all fixed income holdings;
equities are priced using exchange closing prices in their
primary listing market. Any security lacking a reliable
third-party price is subject to a documented fair value
determination by the fund's independent valuation committee.

Counterparty Risk Management: The fund's custodian, Northbridge
Trust & Custody Bank, is subject to annual independent review by
the fund's Operational Risk Committee. Any derivative usage
(limited to interest rate hedging only, not used for leverage) is
subject to counterparty exposure limits and daily collateral
monitoring.

Business Continuity: The fund's administrator maintains a tested
business continuity plan with documented recovery time objectives;
the most recent continuity test was conducted within the trailing
12 months with no material findings requiring escalation.

Conflicts of Interest: The fund's credit research function is
organizationally separate from portfolio management, as described
in Section 4, specifically to mitigate conflicts of interest in
security selection. Any cross-holding between Horizon-managed
funds is disclosed in the annual report.

Regulatory Status: The fund operates under UCITS regulations in
its Luxembourg domicile and is registered for distribution in
multiple jurisdictions; RMs must confirm the fund is registered
for distribution in the client's jurisdiction of residence before
recommending it, as registration status varies by jurisdiction and
is maintained centrally by the Product Governance team.

17. SCENARIO STRESS TESTING SUMMARY (ILLUSTRATIVE)
=============================================================

The portfolio risk team conducts periodic stress testing against
illustrative macroeconomic scenarios to support ongoing risk
oversight. The following scenario summaries are illustrative
modeling exercises, not predictions or guarantees of actual future
fund performance, and must never be presented to clients as
forecasts.

Scenario: Parallel Rate Shock (+150bps): Illustrative modeled
impact to the bond sleeve is a decline of approximately 5.5-6.5%,
partially offset by the equity sleeve's typically lower sensitivity
to rate moves in isolation, for an illustrative blended fund impact
in the range of -4% to -5%, before considering any active
management response.

Scenario: Credit Spread Widening (+100bps investment grade): 
Illustrative modeled impact to the corporate bond sub-sleeve is a
decline of approximately 3-4%, with a smaller blended fund impact
given the corporate sleeve represents roughly a third of total
fund assets.

Scenario: Broad Equity Market Decline (-20%): Given the fund's
defensive equity sleeve characteristics and historical beta of
approximately 0.71 relative to broad equity markets, illustrative
modeled impact to the equity sleeve is a decline in the range of
-13% to -15%, with a blended fund impact in the range of -2.5% to
-3.5% given the equity sleeve's 10-25% weight.

Scenario: Combined Stress (rate shock + credit widening + equity
decline, simultaneous): This combined scenario represents the most
severe illustrative stress case modeled and produces a blended
fund impact broadly consistent with, though not identical to, the
fund's historical maximum drawdown figures cited in Section 5.
Combined scenarios do not simply sum individual scenario impacts
due to imperfect correlation between risk factors; the portfolio
risk team's modeling accounts for historical and stressed
correlation assumptions.

RMs should never present stress test outputs to clients as
predictions of future performance. These are risk management tools
used internally to validate that the fund's actual risk exposure
remains consistent with its stated risk rating and mandate, and may
only be referenced with clients in general, qualitative terms (e.g.,
"the fund is designed to limit downside in adverse rate
environments") rather than with specific modeled percentage figures,
unless Compliance has specifically pre-approved such disclosure for
a sophisticated or institutional client context.
