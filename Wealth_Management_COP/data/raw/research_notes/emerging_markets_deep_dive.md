## Executive Summary

Emerging markets present a bifurcated opportunity in 2026. India and select Southeast Asian markets offer compelling structural growth stories, while China faces ongoing headwinds from property sector deleveraging and geopolitical friction. Latin America remains mixed; commodity-dependent economies face headwinds from lower commodity prices.

---

## 1. India — Our Top EM Conviction

### Macro
- GDP Growth Forecast 2026: +6.8% (IMF estimate)
- Inflation: 4.8% (within RBI comfort zone)
- Current Account: -1.4% of GDP (manageable)
- FX: INR broadly stable; lower EM volatility than peers

### Equity Market
- Nifty 50 Index P/E: 22.1x (premium justified by growth)
- EPS Growth Forecast: +14% for FY2026-27
- Sector strengths: Technology services, financial inclusion, infrastructure

### Recommended Exposure
- Growth clients: Up to 15% India equity allocation
- Aggressive clients: Up to 20%
- Preferred vehicles: India-focused ETF or diversified EM fund with India tilt (30%+)

---

## 2. China — Cautious Approach

### Macro
- GDP Growth: 4.8% (below government 5% target)
- Property sector: Still deleveraging; Evergrande resolution ongoing
- Stimulus: Government fiscal support increasing but structural headwinds remain
- Geopolitical: U.S.-China tech restrictions and tariff uncertainty

### Equity Market
- Hang Seng / CSI 300: Cheaply valued (12x P/E) but value trap risk
- Our View: **Reduce to maximum 10% EM allocation in China**
- Key risk: Further escalation of trade tensions could significantly impair Chinese ADR and H-share values

---

## 3. Southeast Asia — Selective Opportunity

### Vietnam
- Manufacturing FDI beneficiary from China+1 strategies
- Strong export growth; equity market developing but illiquid
- **Suitable for Aggressive clients only** via broad EM fund exposure

### Indonesia
- Commodity-driven; nickel and palm oil
- New government — policy continuity positive
- EM fund exposure acceptable; direct allocation only for Aggressive

---

## 4. Latin America

### Brazil
- High real interest rates (13.75%) offering attractive fixed income
- Equity: Commodity exposure + political uncertainty = caution
- EM bond allocation for Growth/Aggressive clients attractive

### Mexico
- Nearshoring beneficiary; close U.S. economic ties positive
- Equity: Selective; automotive and industrial sectors strong

---

## 5. EM Fixed Income

- EM sovereign bonds (USD-denominated): Yield ~6.5-8.5% — attractive for Aggressive clients
- EM local currency bonds: Higher yield but FX risk; Aggressive clients only
- EM corporate bonds: Selectively attractive; stick to investment grade EM corporates for Growth clients
