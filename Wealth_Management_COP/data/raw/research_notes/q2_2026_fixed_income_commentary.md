## Executive Summary

Fixed income markets are navigating a delicate transition. After two years of rate-driven headwinds, the outlook for bonds improves as the Fed signals potential rate cuts in H2 2026. We recommend modestly extending duration in high-quality fixed income while maintaining a cautious stance on high-yield credit.

**Key Call**: Begin extending core fixed income duration from short (1-3yr) toward medium (3-7yr) as rate cut expectations build.

---

## 1. Interest Rate Outlook

### Current Rates (as of April 2026)
- Fed Funds Rate: 5.00-5.25%
- 2-Year U.S. Treasury Yield: 4.45%
- 10-Year U.S. Treasury Yield: 4.20%
- 30-Year U.S. Treasury Yield: 4.38%

### Yield Curve
The yield curve has normalised from inverted territory (2024-2025) to slightly upward sloping. This indicates reduced recession probability and improving growth outlook.

### Rate Cut Expectations
- Market pricing: 2-3 cuts of 25bps each in H2 2026
- Our view: 1-2 cuts, contingent on inflation trajectory
- Key risk: Sticky services inflation could delay cuts

---

## 2. Investment Grade Corporate Bonds

- Spreads: 105bps over Treasuries (historically tight; indicating strong credit conditions)
- Quality: IG credit fundamentals remain solid; default risk low
- Recommendation: **Moderate overweight** — attractive absolute yield (4.8-5.2%) with manageable risk
- Prefer: Financial sector bonds (strong balance sheets) over consumer cyclicals

---

## 3. High Yield Corporate Bonds

- Spreads: 310bps over Treasuries (below long-term average of 400bps — relatively tight)
- Risk: Compressed spreads leave little room for error; default risk rising in rate-sensitive sectors
- Recommendation: **Underweight** relative to recent allocations
- Suitable: Growth and Aggressive clients only; maximum 15% allocation
- **Warning**: High yield is NOT suitable for Conservative or Balanced clients. Tight spreads increase the risk that losses could be severe in a credit event.

---

## 4. Government Bonds and TIPS

- U.S. Treasuries offer 4.2-4.5% yield — attractive for Conservative and Balanced clients
- TIPS: Real yields at +1.8% — attractive inflation hedge if inflation proves persistent
- Recommendation for Conservative clients: Increase TIPS allocation to 15-20% for inflation protection

---

## 5. Portfolio Recommendations by Profile

### Conservative Clients
- Core bonds: 50-60% (mix of government and IG corporate)
- TIPS: 15-20% (inflation protection)
- Short duration: 10-15% (liquidity)
- High yield: 0% (prohibited)

### Balanced Clients
- Core bonds: 25-35%
- TIPS: 5-10%
- Short duration: 5%
- High yield: Maximum 10% (for aggressive balanced profiles only)

### Growth / Aggressive Clients
- Core bonds: 10-15%
- High yield: 10-20% (growth) / up to 30% (aggressive)
- Consider: Floating rate instruments to benefit from higher-for-longer rates

---

## 6. Key Fixed Income Risks

1. **Inflation re-acceleration**: Would delay rate cuts and hurt duration positions
2. **Credit deterioration**: High yield spreads could widen sharply in a slowdown
3. **Liquidity risk**: Some corporate bond markets have reduced liquidity vs. pre-2020
