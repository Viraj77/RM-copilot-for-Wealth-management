# Q2 2026 Equity Market Outlook

## Document Information
- **Type**: Research
- **Sensitivity**: Public
- **Date**: 2026-04-01
- **Source**: WealthMgmt Research Division — Chief Investment Office

---

## Executive Summary

Global equity markets enter Q2 2026 with cautious optimism following a resilient Q1 performance. The S&P 500 delivered +7.2% in Q1, led by technology and consumer discretionary sectors. However, elevated valuations, persistent core inflation, and geopolitical uncertainty create a complex environment for the remainder of the year.

**Our Outlook**: Neutral-Positive on equities. Favour quality over momentum. Recommend gradual rebalancing for overweight equity positions.

---

## 1. U.S. Equity Market

### Valuations
- S&P 500 P/E ratio: 21.4x (vs. 10-year average of 18.2x) — elevated
- Forward P/E: 19.8x — premium vs. historical norms
- Earnings growth outlook: +8% for FY2026 (consensus)

### Sector Views
| Sector | View | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Technology | Overweight | AI capex cycle continues; strong earnings |
| Healthcare | Overweight | Defensive growth; aging demographics |
| Financials | Neutral | Margin pressure from flattening yield curve |
| Energy | Underweight | Demand uncertainty; ESG headwinds |
| Consumer Staples | Neutral | Defensive but expensive |
| Real Estate | Neutral-Positive | Rate sensitivity easing; income appeal |

### Key Risks
1. Federal Reserve policy reversal (if inflation re-accelerates)
2. Earnings disappointments in rate-sensitive sectors
3. AI capex spending normalisation

---

## 2. International Developed Markets (Europe, Japan)

- **Europe**: Modest recovery; ECB on pause; manufacturing PMI improving
- **Japan**: Yen stabilisation supports corporate earnings; BOJ gradual normalisation
- **Recommendation**: Maintain neutral allocation to international developed markets

---

## 3. Emerging Markets

- **China**: Policy stimulus driving recovery; property sector risk remains
- **India**: Strong GDP growth (+6.8%); structural equity bull market
- **EM Risk**: USD strength and Fed policy uncertainty weigh on EM assets
- **Recommendation**: Selective; favour India and Southeast Asia over China for new allocation

---

## 4. Portfolio Implications

For **Balanced portfolios**: Consider trimming equity allocation from 65% toward 55-60% given elevated U.S. valuations. Rotate into international developed and quality fixed income.

For **Growth/Aggressive portfolios**: Maintain equity overweight but shift mix from high-multiple growth stocks toward quality earnings. Consider adding healthcare exposure.

For **Conservative portfolios**: Maintain minimal equity; any additions should be limited to dividend-focused equity (max 20% of total portfolio).

---

## 5. Key Themes for Q2 2026

1. **AI Infrastructure Build-Out**: Semiconductor and cloud infrastructure companies remain attractive but volatile
2. **Interest Rate Normalisation**: Rate cuts expected H2 2026 — positive for duration assets
3. **Reshoring & Industrial Policy**: U.S. manufacturing revival theme continues
4. **ESG Momentum**: Carbon regulation tightening in EU; ESG-screened funds gaining flows
