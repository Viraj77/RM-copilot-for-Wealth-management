## 1. Market Overview

The cryptocurrency market has matured significantly since 2020, with Bitcoin ETF approval in the U.S. (2024) and growing institutional participation. However, the asset class remains highly volatile, speculative, and subject to complex regulatory risk.

### Current Market Statistics (as of Q1 2026)
- Bitcoin (BTC): ~$92,000 USD; Market cap ~$1.82 trillion
- Ethereum (ETH): ~$3,400 USD; Market cap ~$410 billion
- Total crypto market cap: ~$3.1 trillion
- 12-month BTC volatility: ~55% annualised (vs. ~16% for S&P 500)

---

## 2. Regulatory Landscape

### United States
- SEC has approved Bitcoin spot ETFs (e.g., BlackRock, Fidelity)
- Ethereum ETFs approved
- Crypto classified as commodities under CFTC jurisdiction (primarily)
- Tax treatment: Capital gains (short and long term)

### European Union
- MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) regulation in full effect
- Licensed firms required for crypto services

### Key Regulatory Risk
- Ongoing global regulatory fragmentation
- Potential for adverse tax changes
- Anti-money laundering (AML) requirements increasing

## 3. Risk Profile of Crypto Assets

| Risk Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| Market Volatility | Extreme (50-100% annual swings common) |
| Liquidity Risk | High liquidity for BTC/ETH; very low for altcoins |
| Regulatory Risk | High; regulatory change can cause rapid price impact |
| Custody Risk | High; self-custody requires technical expertise |
| Counterparty Risk | High (exchange failures — FTX precedent) |
| Technology Risk | Smart contract bugs, protocol forks, hacks |

**Overall Risk Classification: Speculative / Very High Risk**

## 5. Client Inquiry Handling

If a client asks about cryptocurrency:
1. Acknowledge the client's interest professionally
2. Explain that this is a specialist area requiring licensed advice
3. Document the inquiry in the client file
4. Escalate to the licensed advisor within 24 hours
5. Do NOT share price targets, forecasts, or buy/sell opinions

## 6. Due Diligence on Crypto Products (for Licensed Advisor Use)

If a licensed advisor is evaluating a crypto product for an Aggressive client:
- Use regulated, institutional-grade custodians only (not retail exchanges)
- Prefer regulated ETF wrappers (e.g., BlackRock Bitcoin ETF) over direct crypto custody
- Maximum position: 5% of total portfolio
- Must be documented in client suitability file with explicit risk acknowledgement
