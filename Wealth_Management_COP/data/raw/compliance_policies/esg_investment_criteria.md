## 1. Overview of ESG Investing

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) investing integrates non-financial factors into portfolio construction and security analysis. ESG criteria help identify companies with sustainable business practices, lower regulatory risk, and stronger long-term prospects.

Our firm supports ESG integration across all client portfolios where suitable.

---

## 2. ESG Screening Criteria

### 2.1 Negative Screening (Exclusions)
The following sectors/activities are excluded from our ESG-compliant products:
- Tobacco production and distribution
- Controversial weapons (cluster munitions, landmines, biological/chemical weapons)
- Coal power generation (>30% revenue from thermal coal)
- Gambling operations
- Adult entertainment

### 2.2 Positive Screening (Inclusions)
Priority given to companies with:
- Carbon reduction targets aligned with Paris Agreement (1.5°C pathway)
- Strong board diversity (≥40% gender diversity at board level)
- Transparent supply chain and human rights policies
- Third-party ESG ratings of BB or above (MSCI ESG Rating scale)

### 2.3 ESG Integration
For non-exclusionary ESG integration, ESG factors are weighted alongside traditional financial metrics in security analysis.

## 3. Available ESG Products

### 3.1 ESG Global Equity Fund
- **Benchmark**: MSCI World ESG Leaders Index
- **Strategy**: ESG-screened global equities
- **Expense Ratio**: 0.20%
- **Suitable Profiles**: Balanced, Growth, Aggressive
- **3-Year Annualized Return**: 10.1%

### 3.2 Green Bond Fund
- **Benchmark**: Bloomberg MSCI Global Green Bond Index
- **Strategy**: Bonds funding environmental projects
- **Expense Ratio**: 0.15%
- **Suitable Profiles**: All profiles
- **3-Year Annualized Return**: 2.8%

---

## 4. ESG Suitability

RMs should discuss ESG preferences with clients during KYC. Client preferences for ESG should be documented. ESG restrictions may limit universe and potentially affect returns. Clients must acknowledge this trade-off in writing when selecting ESG-screened products.

---

## 5. Reporting

ESG-invested portfolios receive an annual ESG impact report covering:
- Carbon footprint vs. benchmark
- SDG alignment (Sustainable Development Goals)
- Engagement and voting activities by fund managers
