# Conservative Portfolio Suitability Policy

## Document Information
- **Type**: Policy
- **Sensitivity**: Internal
- **Date**: 2026-01-01
- **Source**: WealthMgmt Compliance & Suitability Division

---

## 1. Definition of Conservative Risk Profile

A Conservative client is one who:
- Prioritises capital preservation over growth
- Has a low tolerance for portfolio value fluctuations
- Typically has a short to medium investment horizon (≤5 years)
- May be approaching or in retirement
- Would be significantly distressed by a loss of more than 10% of portfolio value

---

## 2. Permitted Investments for Conservative Clients

### 2.1 Fixed Income (Required: 50%-80% of portfolio)
- Investment-grade bonds only (BBB- or higher rating)
- Government bonds: No limit within fixed income allocation
- Corporate bonds: Maximum 30% of total portfolio
- High yield / sub-investment grade: STRICTLY PROHIBITED
- Duration: Prefer short to medium duration (≤7 years average)

### 2.2 Equity (Maximum: 25% of portfolio)
- Dividend-focused equity funds only (e.g., DIEF)
- No sector-concentrated or thematic equity funds
- No individual stock picking
- Technology Growth Fund (TGF): STRICTLY PROHIBITED
- Emerging markets equity: STRICTLY PROHIBITED

### 2.3 Alternatives (Maximum: 0%)
- All alternative investments are PROHIBITED for Conservative clients
- REITs, hedge funds, private credit, commodities: NOT PERMITTED
- Exception: Listed REIT ETFs may be considered up to 5% with supervisor approval only

### 2.4 Cash & Money Market (Required: Minimum 10%)
- Money market funds and high-grade short-term instruments are encouraged
- Cash provides liquidity buffer and capital protection

---

## 3. Prohibited Recommendations for Conservative Clients

The following recommendations are NEVER suitable for Conservative clients:
1. High Yield Corporate Bond Fund (HYCBF)
2. Technology Growth Fund (TGF)
3. ARK Innovation ETF or similar thematic/concentrated funds
4. Any cryptocurrency or digital asset product
5. Private credit or illiquid alternative funds
6. Leveraged or inverse ETFs
7. Any product with lock-up periods exceeding 6 months
8. Emerging markets equity funds

---

## 4. Escalation Requirements

RMs must escalate to a licensed financial advisor before:
- Any recommendation that increases equity above 20%
- Any recommendation of alternative investments for conservative clients
- Any request from a conservative client to make aggressive investments ("client override")

Client preference does NOT override suitability rules. If a conservative client insists on unsuitable products, the RM must document the conversation and escalate to compliance.
